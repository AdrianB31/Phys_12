The portable biological incubator is essential for detecting bacterial disease for medical spaces in rural areas. As a result, the device consists of a 3D printed cast to hold petri plates, a heat source that incubates the plate to 40C, as well as a carbon dioxide and oxygen gas sensor that measures the gas concentration in the incubator. Furthermore, the 3D printed cast would be separated into two chambers (one for oxygen and the other for carbon dioxide incubation). Controlling the gas levels is critical for regulating the pH of the media on the petri plates to ensure repeatable success of microbial growth, enzyme function, and the stability of cell structure.
